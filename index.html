Our Diary

I’m not here to convince you or pull you anywhere. I just wanted you to hear *me* — quietly, without noise.

Texts feel rushed sometimes. Conversations get mixed with too many opinions. This is just me, slowing things down, once.

There’s something I’ve always felt about us. Even when we step away, we somehow find our way back. Not out of habit — but out of comfort, familiarity, and something that still feels real.

I just want you to see what’s right in front of you — two people who cared deeply, who couldn’t let go easily, and one person here who has finally understood a lot of things.

I’m not saying we were perfect. We weren’t. But I see my mistakes clearly now — and I genuinely want to fix what I once didn’t know how to handle.

Letting go can feel lighter in the moment. But some things deserve one honest pause, one real look, before being left behind.

I grew up hearing stories where love wasn’t rushed. Where even Shiv didn’t chase or force anything — he worked on himself, faced his fears, and became steady.

That story stayed with me. Love doesn’t return because you beg for it — it returns when you become safe enough to hold it.

I understand why you’re scared. Honestly, I would be too.

I don’t expect trust immediately. I don’t expect belief without time.

I choose not to give up — not by forcing anything, but by learning, changing, and showing up better than before.

All I ask is this — just look into this once, when you feel ready. Nothing more than that.

Distance scared me once too. Then I realised something.

Achievements feel a little empty when the person who believed in you isn’t there to hear about them.

Distance doesn’t weaken real things. It only shows what already mattered.

And if you ever felt tired of swimming in thoughts, you wouldn’t need to decide anything. Just a place to sit, breathe, and rest for a moment.

I just wanted you to know where I stand — calmly, honestly, without pulling you anywhere.
